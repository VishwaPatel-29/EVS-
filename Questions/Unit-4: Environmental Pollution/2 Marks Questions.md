# 🌍 Environmental Science (EVS)

## 📚 Unit 4: Environmental Pollution

### 📝 Short Questions (2 Marks Each)

---

# ❓ Question 1

## Define environmental pollution.

### ✅ Answer

Environmental pollution is the undesirable change in the physical, chemical, or biological characteristics of the environment due to the introduction of harmful substances called pollutants. It adversely affects living organisms and ecosystems.

---

# ❓ Question 2

## What is air pollution?

### ✅ Answer

Air pollution is the contamination of the atmosphere by harmful gases, smoke, dust, and other pollutants that negatively affect human health, living organisms, and the environment.

---

# ❓ Question 3

## Mention any two sources of air pollution.

### ✅ Answer

### 🌫️ Sources of Air Pollution:

1. 🚗 Vehicular emissions
2. 🏭 Industrial emissions

Other sources include burning of fossil fuels and forest fires.

---

# ❓ Question 4

## State two effects of air pollution.

### ✅ Answer

### ⚠️ Effects of Air Pollution:

1. 🫁 Respiratory diseases such as asthma and bronchitis.
2. 🌡️ Global warming and climate change.

---

# ❓ Question 5

## What is water pollution?

### ✅ Answer

Water pollution is the contamination of rivers, lakes, oceans, groundwater, and other water bodies by harmful substances, making the water unsafe for human use and aquatic life.

---

# ❓ Question 6

## Mention two sources of water pollution.

### ✅ Answer

### 💧 Sources of Water Pollution:

1. 🏭 Industrial effluents
2. 🚰 Domestic sewage

Other sources include agricultural runoff and oil spills.

---

# ❓ Question 7

## Define soil pollution.

### ✅ Answer

Soil pollution is the contamination of soil by harmful chemicals, pesticides, industrial waste, and other pollutants, reducing its fertility and affecting living organisms.

---

# ❓ Question 8

## State two causes of soil pollution.

### ✅ Answer

### 🌱 Causes of Soil Pollution:

1. ☣️ Excessive use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides.
2. 🏭 Disposal of industrial and municipal waste on land.

---

# ❓ Question 9

## What is noise pollution?

### ✅ Answer

Noise pollution refers to excessive or unwanted sound that causes discomfort, stress, and adverse effects on human health and the environment.

---

# ❓ Question 10

## Mention any two sources of noise pollution.

### ✅ Answer

### 🔊 Sources of Noise Pollution:

1. 🚗 Road traffic and vehicle horns.
2. 🏭 Industrial machinery and construction activities.

---

# ❓ Question 11

## State two harmful effects of noise pollution.

### ✅ Answer

### ⚠️ Harmful Effects:

1. 👂 Hearing loss and ear-related problems.
2. 😣 Stress, anxiety, and sleep disturbances.

---

# ❓ Question 12

## What is meant by pollution control?

### ✅ Answer

Pollution control refers to the measures and techniques used to prevent, reduce, or eliminate environmental pollution and protect human health and ecosystems.

---

# ❓ Question 13

## Name any two methods of air pollution control.

### ✅ Answer

### 🌫️ Air Pollution Control Methods:

1. 🌳 Afforestation and plantation of trees.
2. 🏭 Installation of filters and scrubbers in industries.

---

# ❓ Question 14

## Mention two measures for preventing soil pollution.

### ✅ Answer

### 🌱 Measures to Prevent Soil Pollution:

1. ♻️ Proper disposal and recycling of waste materials.
2. 🌿 Reducing the use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides.

---

# ❓ Question 15

## What is wastewater treatment?

### ✅ Answer

Wastewater treatment is the process of removing pollutants, contaminants, and harmful substances from sewage and industrial wastewater before releasing it into the environment or reusing it.
