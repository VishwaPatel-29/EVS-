# 🌍 Environmental Science (EVS)
## 📚 Unit 2: Ecosystems and Biodiversity
### 📝 Short Questions (2 Marks Each)

---

# ❓ Question 1
## Define ecosystem.

### ✅ Answer

An ecosystem is a functional unit of nature where living organisms (**biotic components**) interact with each other and with the non-living environment (**abiotic components**). These interactions help in the flow of energy and cycling of nutrients. Examples include forests, ponds, and grasslands.

---

# ❓ Question 2
## What are biotic components of an ecosystem?

### ✅ Answer

Biotic components are the living organisms present in an ecosystem. They include:

- 🌱 Plants
- 🐾 Animals
- 🍄 Fungi
- 🦠 Bacteria and microorganisms

These organisms interact with one another and contribute to the functioning of the ecosystem.

---

# ❓ Question 3
## What are abiotic components of an ecosystem?

### ✅ Answer

Abiotic components are the non-living physical and chemical factors of an ecosystem. They include:

- ☀️ Sunlight
- 💧 Water
- 🌬️ Air
- 🪨 Soil
- 🌡️ Temperature
- ⚗️ Minerals

These factors influence the growth and survival of living organisms.

---

# ❓ Question 4
## Define producers with an example.

### ✅ Answer

Producers are organisms that prepare their own food through the process of photosynthesis using sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide. They form the first trophic level in a food chain and provide energy to other organisms.

### 🌱 Example:
- Green plants
- Algae

---

# ❓ Question 5
## Define consumers with an example.

### ✅ Answer

Consumers are organisms that cannot prepare their own food and depend on other organisms for nutrition. They obtain energy by feeding on plants or animals.

### 🐾 Example:
- Deer
- Lion
- Human beings

---

# ❓ Question 6
## What are decomposers?

### ✅ Answer

Decomposers are organisms that break down dead plants, animals, and organic waste into simpler substances. They help recycle nutrients back into the environment and maintain ecological balance.

### 🍄 Example:
- Fungi
- Bacteria

---

# ❓ Question 7
## What is a food chain?

### ✅ Answer

A food chain is a sequence of organisms through which energy and nutrients pass as one organism eats another. It shows the feeding relationship among organisms in an ecosystem.

### 🌿 Example:

```text
Grass → Deer → Tiger
```

---

# ❓ Question 8
## What is a food web?

### ✅ Answer

A food web is a network of interconnected food chains in an ecosystem. It shows multiple feeding relationships among organisms and provides stability to the ecosystem.

### 🌍 Importance:
- Maintains ecological balance
- Increases ecosystem stability
- Provides alternative food sources

---

# ❓ Question 9
## Define ecological pyramid.

### ✅ Answer

An ecological pyramid is a graphical representation of the number, biomass, or energy of organisms at different trophic levels in an ecosystem. It helps in understanding the flow of energy and ecological relationships.

---

# ❓ Question 10
## Name the three types of ecological pyramids.

### ✅ Answer

The three types of ecological pyramids are:

1. 🔢 Pyramid of Numbers
2. ⚖️ Pyramid of Biomass
3. ⚡ Pyramid of Energy

These pyramids represent different aspects of ecosystem structure and functioning.

---

# ❓ Question 11
## What is a forest ecosystem?

### ✅ Answer

A forest ecosystem is a natural ecosystem dominated by trees and other vegetation. It consists of plants, animals, microorganisms, soil, and climate interacting with one another. Forests play an important role in maintaining biodiversity and ecological balance.

### 🌳 Examples:
- Tropical Forests
- Temperate Forests
- Coniferous Forests

---

# ❓ Question 12
## Define grassland ecosystem.

### ✅ Answer

A grassland ecosystem is an ecosystem where grasses are the dominant vegetation and very few trees are present. It supports a variety of herbivores, carnivores, and microorganisms and is found in regions with moderate rainfall.

### 🌾 Examples:
- Prairies
- Savannas
- Steppes

---

# ❓ Question 13
## What is biodiversity?

### ✅ Answer

Biodiversity refers to the variety and variability of living organisms present on Earth. It includes diversity among plants, animals, microorganisms, and the ecosystems in which they live. Biodiversity is essential for maintaining ecological balance.

---

# ❓ Question 14
## Mention any two types of biodiversity.

### ✅ Answer

### 🧬 Genetic Diversity
Variation in genes within a species.

### 🐾 Species Diversity
Variety of different species present in an ecosystem.

### 🌍 Another Type:
- Ecosystem Diversity

---

# ❓ Question 15
## State any two methods of biodiversity conservation.

### ✅ Answer

### 🌳 In-situ Conservation
Protecting species in their natural habitats.

**Examples:**
- National Parks
- Wildlife Sanctuaries
- Biosphere Reserves

### 🏛️ Ex-situ Conservation
Protecting species outside their natural habitats.

**Examples:**
- Zoos
- Botanical Gardens
- Seed Banks
